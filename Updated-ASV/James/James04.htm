1 Whence come wars and whence come fightings among you? come they not hence, even of your pleasures that war in your members? 2 You lust, and have not: you kill, and covet, and cannot obtain: you fight and war; you have not, because you ask not. 3 You ask, and receive not, because you ask amiss, that you may spend it in your pleasures. 4 You adulteresses, know you not that the friendship of the world is enmity with God? Whosoever therefore would be a friend of the world maketh himself an enemy of God. 5 Or think you that the scripture speaketh in vain? Doth the spirit which he made to dwell in us long to envying? 6 But he giveth more grace. Wherefore the scripture saith, God resisteth the proud, but giveth grace to the humble. 7 Be subject therefore to God; but resist the devil, and he will flee from you. 8 Draw nigh to God, and he will draw nigh to you. Cleanse your hands, you sinners; and purify your hearts, you doubleminded. 9 Be afflicted, and mourn, and weep: let your laughter be turned to mourning, and your joy to heaviness. 10 Humble yourselves in the sight of the Lord, and he will exalt you.
11 Speak not one against another, brethren. He that speaketh against a brother, or judgeth his brother, speaketh against the law, and judgeth the law: but if you judgest the law, you are not a doer of the law, but a judge. 12 One only is the lawgiver and judge, even he who is able to save and to destroy: but who art you that judgest your neighbor?
13 Come now, you that say, To-day or to-morrow we will go into this city, and spend a youar there, and trade, and get gain: 14 whereas you know not what will be on the morrow. What is your life? For you are a vapor that appeareth for a little time, and then vanisheth away. 15 For that you ought to say, If the Lord will, we will both live, and do this or that. 16 But now you glory in your vauntings: all such glorying is evil. 17 To him therefore that knoweth to do good, and doeth it not, to him it is sin.
