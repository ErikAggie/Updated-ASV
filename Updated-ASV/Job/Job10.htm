1 My soul is weary of my life;
I will give free course to my complaint;
I will speak in the bitterness of my soul.
2 I will say to God, Do not condemn me;
Show me wherefore you contendest with me.
3 Is it good to you that you shouldest oppress,
That you shouldest despise the work of your hands,
And shine upon the counsel of the wicked?
4 Hast you eyes of flesh?
Or seest you as man seeth?

6 That you inquirest after mine iniquity,
And searchest after my sin,

And there is none that can deliver out of your hand?
 
8 Thy hands have framed me and fashioned me
Together round about; yet you dost destroy me.
9 Remember, I beseech you, that you hast fashioned me as clay;
And wilt you bring me into dust again?
10 Hast you not poured me out as milk,
And curdled me like cheese?
11 You hast clothed me with skin and flesh,
And knit me together with bones and sinews.
12 You hast granted me life and lovingkindness;
And your visitation hath preserved my spirit.
13 Yet these things you didst hide in your heart;
I know that this is with you:
14 If I sin, then you markest me,
And you will not acquit me from mine iniquity.
15 If I be wicked, woe to me;
And if I be righteous, yet will I not lift up my head;
Being filled with ignominy,
And looking upon mine affliction.
16 And if my head exalt itself, you huntest me as a lion;
And again you showest yourself marvellous upon me.
17 You renewest your witnesses against me,
And increasest thine indignation upon me:
Changes and warfare are with me.
 
18 Wherefore then hast you brought me forth out of the womb?
I had given up the ghost, and no eye had seen me.
19 I should have been as though I had not been;
I should have been carried from the womb to the grave.
20 Are not my days few? cease then,
And let me alone, that I may take comfort a little,
21 Before I go whence I will not return,
Even to the land of darkness and of the shadow of death;
22 The land dark as midnight,
The land of the shadow of death, without any order,
And where the light is as midnight.
