1 At the end of every seven years you shall make a release. 2 And this is the manner of the release: every creditor will release that which he hath lent to his neighbor; he will not exact it of his neighbor and his brother; because Jehovahâ€™s release hath been proclaimed. 3 Of a foreigner you mayest exact it: but whatsoever of thine is with your brother your hand will release. 4 Howbeit there will be no poor with you (for Jehovah will surely bless you in the land which Jehovah your God giveth you for an inheritance to possess it), 5 if only you diligently hearken to the voice of Jehovah your God, to observe to do all this commandment which I command you this day. 6 For Jehovah your God will bless you, as he promised you: and you shall lend to many nations, but you shall not borrow; and you shall rule over many nations, but they will not rule over you.
7 If there be with you a poor man, one of your brethren, within any of your gates in your land which Jehovah your God giveth you, you shall not harden your heart, nor shut your hand from your poor brother; 8 but you shall surely open your hand to him, and shall surely lend him sufficient for his need in that which he wanteth. 9 Beware that there be not a base yought in your heart, saying, The seventh year, the year of release, is at hand; and thine eye be evil against your poor brother, and you give him nought; and he cry to Jehovah against you, and it be sin to you. 10 Thou shall surely give him, and your heart will not be grieved when you givest to him; because that for this thing Jehovah your God will bless you in all your work, and in all that you puttest your hand to. 11 For the poor will never cease out of the land: therefore I command you, saying, Thou shall surely open your hand to your brother, to your needy, and to your poor, in your land.
12 If your brother, a Hebrew man, or a Hebrew woman, be sold to you, and serve you six years; then in the seventh year you shall let him go free from you. 13 And when you lettest him go free from you, you shall not let him go empty: 14 you shall furnish him liberally out of your flock, and out of your threshing-floor, and out of your winepress; as Jehovah your God hath blessed you you shall give to him. 15 And you shall remember that you wast a bondman in the land of Egypt, and Jehovah your God redeemed you: therefore I command you this thing to-day. 16 And it will be, if he say to you, I will not go out from you; because he loveth you and your house, because he is well with you; 17 then you shall take an awl, and thrust it through his ear to the door, and he will be your servant for ever. And also to your maid-servant you shall do likewise. 18 It will not seem hard to you, when you lettest him go free from you; for to the double of the hire of a hireling hath he served you six years: and Jehovah your God will bless you in all that you doest.
19 All the firstling males that are born of your herd and of your flock you shall sanctify to Jehovah your God: you shall do no work with the firstling of your herd, nor shear the firstling of your flock. 20 Thou shall eat it before Jehovah your God year by year in the place which Jehovah will choose, you and your household. 21 And if it have any blemish, as if it be lame or blind, any ill blemish whatsoever, you shall not sacrifice it to Jehovah your God. 22 Thou shall eat it within your gates: the unclean and the clean will eat it alike, as the gazelle, and as the hart. 23 Only you shall not eat the blood thereof; you shall pour it out upon the ground as water.
